Fresh Southern Peach Cobbler

Description

The peach cobbler is an absolute classic and a guaranteed hit when peaches are in season. This recipe in particular is a reader favorite, so we encourage you to give it a shot even if cobblers aren't quite your thing.

This recipe should take around an hour to make.

Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 8 fresh peaches - peeled, pitted and sliced into thin wedges
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup boiling water
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425 degree F (220 degrees C)
  2. In a large bowl, combine peaches, 1/4 cup white sugar, 1/4 cup brown sugar, 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon, nutmeg, lemon juice, and cornstarch. Toss to coat evenly.
  3. Pour mixture into a 2 quart baking dish.
  4. Bake in preheated oven for 10 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, in a large bowl, combine flour, 1/4 cup white sugar, 1/4 cup brown sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  6. Blend in butter with your fingertips or a pastry blender until mixture resembles coarse meal.
  7. Stir in water until just combined.
  8. Remove peaches from oven and drop spoonfuls of topping over them.
  9. Mix 3 tablespoons white sugar with 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon.
  10. Sprinkle cinnamon mixture over entire cobbler.
  11. Bake until topping is golden, about 30 minutes.
